Forza Horizon 6 is the latest entry in the Forza Horizon series of games and it really does not disappoint in my opinion. The location this time is Japan. The map is definitely a lot better from the previous entry, Forza Horizon 5 which was set in Mexico, which felt very flat and the same. Japan is very unique and diverse no matter where you go.

Racing is the core of Forza Horizon 6, and it’s where the game feels the most alive. Whether it’s street races through tight city corners or long highway sprints, every event pushes you to learn the roads and get comfortable with your car. I’ve been bouncing between different race types just to see how each one feels, and the variety keeps things from getting repetitive.

When I’m not running full races, I usually end up drifting, testing builds on the drag strips, or exploring off‑road. Drifting feels like its own side activity — the map has plenty of long corners and mountain roads that make it easy to practice slides or experiment with different setups. The three drag strips (¼ mile, ½ mile, and 1 km) are great for comparing acceleration and top‑speed builds, but they’re also some of the busiest spots in the game. Along with the time trials, these areas are where the world feels most alive, since players naturally gather there to test cars, race each other, or just hang out. Off‑roading is another thing I end up doing a lot, especially because almost every tree — except the cherry blossoms — is destructible, which makes cutting across forests and fields surprisingly fun. Altogether, it gives me plenty to do between the main races.
